Break-glass codes

Physical QR cards that grant temporary, group-scoped emergency access. Every scan is logged.

How break-glass access works

Scanning a card opens its link and immediately starts a short-lived session — no email, login, or two-factor step. The scan itself is the credential, so cards must stay physically controlled. Every scan is logged and admins are notified.

A card carries exactly one group — its target group — and nothing else. It does not grant admin access, it can't satisfy an app that allows by email domain, and it can't open a plain protected route. Emergency access has to be opted into explicitly, per app.

To scope a card to a particular app: set its target group to a group that app's Caddy snippet requires (groups=…). The card then opens only the apps whose policy lists that group. Give each emergency entry point its own group so one card can't open more than you intend.
